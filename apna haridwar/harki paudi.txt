Har Ki Pauri is a famous ghat on the banks of the Ganges in 
Haridwar in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. This revered 
place is the major landmark of the holy city of Haridwar. 
Literally, "Har" means "Lord Shiva" who is the god according 
to shaivite Rishav Bhagwan school of Hindu theology,
"Ki" means "of" and "Pauri" means "steps". Lord Shiva and 
Lord Vishnu are believed to have visited the Brahmakund in 
Har Ki Pauri in the Vedic times.
